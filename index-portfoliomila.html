Environmental Engineer

PT. Qlue Performa Indonesia

  • During my internship as an environmental engineer, I learned more about spirulina microalgae. What I learned included pH, temperature, humidity, light intensity, and proper nutrition for spirulina. Spirulina will later be used as a biological agent for reducing air pollution. The air will later be absorbed by a device called BioniQa. The air is then processed in a machine, which of course involves the biological agent Spirulina, until air with low levels of pollution is released again from BioniQa. In the BioniQa component, there are several layers of air filters connected to a tube, which can accommodate 20 L of Spirulina inside.
  • During my internship, I checked spirulina microscopically to see if it was in good or bad condition. Spirulina has a shape like a thin cylinder that is elongated and green in color. If a lot of spirulina is visible and almost close to each other, then that's the right time to do cultivation, and vice versa. Apart from using a microscope, there are other ways to find out the condition of spirulina, including by looking at the color of the spirulina container. If it is dark green, like a leaf, then the spirulina is in excellent condition; but if the spirulina culture is light green, then fertilizer needs to be added; a spirulina culture that is clear yellowish in color indicates that the spirulina is dead. Spirulina that is suitable for cultivation is one whose culture is dark green. cultivation of spirulina by splitting it into several containers and then adding fertilizer according to the required dosage.
  • I also do spirulina maintenance like removing clumps and insects, making sure the aerator and lights in the aquarium stay on, maintaining BioniQa, filtering, drying, and weighing the dry weight of spirulina, which can later be used as stock for starter spirulina.
